THE PROBLEM

When Infrastructure Falls, Lives Are On The Line

In flood-prone regions like Bangladesh, disaster response teams face impossible coordination challenges.

01
No Internet, No Cellular
Cell towers flood. Fiber lines snap. Teams lose all ability to coordinate supply movement across affected regions.
02
Roads Flood Unpredictably
Routes that worked hours ago become impassable. Static routing plans fail. Deliveries stall or go missing entirely.
03
Supply Diversion & No Accountability
Without cryptographic proof-of-delivery, critical supplies get diverted. There is no tamper-proof audit trail.
02 / 08
OUR SOLUTION

A Platform That Works When Nothing Else Does

Digital Delta coordinates disaster relief entirely offline — syncing via Bluetooth mesh, resolving conflicts mathematically, and predicting floods with ML.

📴
Offline-First
Every operation works without internet. SQLite on-device, CRDT conflict resolution, local-first architecture.
📡
Peer-to-Peer
BLE mesh networking with store-and-forward relay. Devices sync directly — no servers needed in the field.
🧠
AI-Powered
ML model trained on real Bangladesh flood data predicts route decay at 98.3% accuracy. Proactive rerouting saves lives.
🔒
Crypto-Secure
Ed25519 signatures, TOTP auth, hash-chained audit logs, and cryptographic proof-of-delivery. Zero trust by design.
